What could a million people build together?

Technology is urging us to collaborate, however there is an antiquated fear holding us back.

Massive collaboration requires openness, and companies fear open innovation. This relegates collaboration to highly fragmented efforts and poorly funded nonprofits.

Openly collaborative efforts democratize the process of innovation and lower its costs. These efforts firstmost seek investments of labor not capital. Led by convictions in their mission they create transcendent companies.

Our goal is to facilitate the launch of companies which innovate openly. We have developed a simple system to make this happen and it is detailed on the rest of the page.

We hope to empower the world to dream ambitiously, and we want you to join us.

Inception

At the inception of an idea, the founder writes out a detailed proposal of the project. He or she then may privately invite co-founders to polish off the idea and prepare the project for others to work on. When ready the founders proceed to attracting a workforce.

Work

When a project is revealed publicly its work is split up into general tasks. Tasks are delegated to anyone who is willing to contribute a solution. Each solution may contain its own set of tasks, creating a tree of tasks and solutions. In order for a solution to create its own tasks it must be accepted by the parent task. Acceptance of a solution is meant to focus the efforts of the community, and to keep the task tree from sprawling out of control.

A solution is designated a branch name in each of the repositories of the project, and only the solution creator has push access to the particular branch. On completion, a peer review determines the value of a given solution. The work being done on each branch can be merged up at any point from any of the solutions, making the work largely asynchronous.

The review results in two important metrics : impact and acceptance. Impact is analogous to value and acceptance is an impact-weighted percentage of acceptance. If acceptance is below a certain threshold, the user won’t be rewarded impact for the solution unless things are resolved. To the person being reviewed votes are visible only as rejections and acceptances by a particular user, the details of each vote is held private.

Comments left in code reviews and discussions are also voted on, determining an impact for each contribution in the development process.

Compensation

Impact will always be backed by a pool of company stock, like an employee pool in regular companies. As work is completed on the project, users will accumulate impact through the review process. The impact the user accumulates can be seen as the amount of work he or she has done for the company. The total impact, a sum of all the impacts accumulated by all the users on the project, can be viewed as the total amount of work to get the project to that particular state.

Periodically users will be able to exchange a portion of their impact for company stock. The exchange rate is determined by dividing the size of the remaining backing stock pool by the total non-exchanged impact at the time of the exchange event. The periodicity of the exchange events rewards people who accumulated the most impact earliest in the project’s development by allowing them to exchange at higher rates.

At any point the company’s board, which is initially just the founders, can decide to exchange the remaining backing pool at the current exchange rate or to allocate more stock for the backing pool. The board cannot perform any action that would reduce the total value of the backing pool.

Financing

To initiate a round of financing the board, must submit a plan with the amount of stock that will be sold and the minimum acceptable pre-money valuation for the company. A simple yay or nay vote from the project participants will determine an impact-weighted acceptance for the financing round.

If financing is approved, project participants will bid blindly by submitting valuations coupled with the amount of stock they wish to purchase. With impact act as a tiebreaker, the stock will be distributed amongst the bidders to provide the maximum valuation possible. Afterwards if some of the originally provisioned stock remains, the board may decide to have a follow up round with outside investors.

There are currently SEC regulations preventing the sale of stock in privately held companies to non-accredited investors, so the first round of financing described above may not be possible, however we will investigate legal ways around this. If all else fails capital will be raised from a network of accredited investors.

Specifics

This project, which you have been invited to join, is structured like so :

85% of the company’s equity is backing impact, the other 15% is designated to founders and angel investors. Annually on the anniversary of the public release of the site 25% of non-exchanged impact will be available to exchange for stock.

Before the public release, as an early participant, you will also have the opportunity to submit your own ideas and become founders.
